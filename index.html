Welcome to the EEG Neurofeedback Laboratory

The long term aim of the EEG Neurofeedback Lab is to develop the basic knowledge about electroencephlagraphy (EEG) needed to develop novel brain-computer interface approaches to augment assessment and intervention in stuttering. In cooperation with other labs working on similar goals, we've been focused on describing basic differences in EEG signals in various speech perception, production, and working memory tasks primarily in adults who stutter. The lab makes use of EEG equipment along with analysis of electromyogrpahic (EMG) and autonomic signals toward the long term aims. While lab goals are focused on stuttering, our work intersects with basic research in many other areas including motor control, language, working memory, and interoception.

The lab is led by Dr. Andrew Bowers, Associate Professor at the University of Arkansas and is one of a group of collaborating EEG labs.Other labs,students, and collaborations are highlighted on the blog page.
