Welcome to the EEG Neurofeedback Laboratory

The long term aim of the EEG Neurofeedback Lab is to develop the basic knowledge about electroencephalography (EEG) needed to develop novel brain-computer interface approaches to intervention in stuttering. EEG is just a noninvasive way of recording electrical signals generated by the brain with electrodes placed on the scalp surface. In cooperation with other labs working on similar goals, we've been focused on describing basic differences in EEG signals in various speech perception, production, and working memory tasks primarily in adults who stutter. We use EEG equipment along with analysis of electromyography (EMG) and autonomic signals (like heart rate) toward the long term aims. While lab goals are focused on stuttering, our work intersects with basic research in many other areas including motor control, language, working memory, and interoception. Like the signals shown in the figure above, the EEG is composed of separable components that inhibit processing (orange-yellow) and other signals that facilitate it (blue-green). Both kinds of signals contribute to how we process the world and act within it. As shown in the figure to the left, these separable signals also peak at different times and frequencies (darker blue or orange colors). We want to disentangle the contributions of those signals to cognition and behavior and determine how they interact with the body to identify reliable biomarkers of stuttering. We believe that once we can identify these biomarkers, we may be able to develop brain-based feedback protocols to enhance therapy in stuttering. Please see the About page for more information about lab goals, people, and the Projects page for what we are working on now.
