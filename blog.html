Neurofeedback for Stuttering: Current Progress and Future Directions

Andrew Bowers May 19, 2025 5 Comments Research Updates

Our lab has been making significant strides in the development and testing of neurofeedback protocols for developmental stuttering. In this post, I'd like to share some of our recent progress and discuss where we see this research heading in the next few years.

The premise of neurofeedback for stuttering is to give individuals real-time feedback about their brain activity, specifically sensorimotor beta oscillations (15-30 Hz) that have been implicated in speech motor control. By visualizing these oscillations and providing rewards for specific patterns, participants can learn to voluntarily modulate their brain activity in ways that may support more fluent speech production.

Beta Burst Analysis: A New Approach to Understanding Speech Motor Control

Andrew Bowers April 28, 2025 8 Comments Research Methods

Traditional approaches to EEG analysis often focus on sustained oscillatory activity averaged over time. However, recent research has highlighted the importance of transient oscillatory events, particularly in the beta frequency band. These short-lived "bursts" of activity may provide crucial insights into the dynamic nature of speech motor control.

In our lab, we've been developing new methods for detecting and characterizing beta bursts in EEG recordings during speech production tasks. These methods allow us to examine not just the average power in a frequency band, but the specific timing, amplitude, and duration of individual burst events.

Conference Recap: International Conference on Neurofeedback 2025

Andrew Bowers April 15, 2025 3 Comments Conferences

Last month, our lab had the opportunity to present our latest findings at the International Conference on Neurofeedback in Berlin. This gathering of researchers, clinicians, and technologists provided valuable insights into the current state of the field and emerging trends in neurofeedback applications.

Our presentation on "Beta Burst Dynamics in Developmental Stuttering" generated significant interest and discussion. We shared preliminary results from our ongoing study examining how beta burst features differ between adults who stutter and fluent speakers during speech preparation and execution.
